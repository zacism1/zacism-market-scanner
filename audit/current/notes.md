# Zacism Market Scanner — current-state audit

Captured 2 July 2026 from the local site at desktop and 390 × 844 phone viewports.

1. Desktop home — healthy foundation. The Owner’s Ledger has a distinctive professional voice, the Top 5 hierarchy is clear, and published research is clickable. The research table is intentionally dense and its later columns need careful responsive treatment.
2. Mobile home — readable, but incomplete as a site experience. The first screen adapts cleanly; however, there is no proper menu, no direct route to Options or History from the header, and the only section navigation is an offscreen horizontal rail further down the page.
3. Published sections — the tab rail works, but it does not communicate that more destinations exist outside the viewport. It also lacks explicit tab/selected-state semantics.
4. Investment Mode — good deep-report detail, but core ideas and 10x/asymmetric ideas share one undifferentiated stream. The latter needs its own heading and explanatory context.
5. History — the Archive action stores editions only in the visitor’s browser. It cannot show real weekly publications to a new visitor or across devices; publisher-backed edition files are the appropriate source of truth.
6. Options — the detail model supports expiry, legs, entry, profit exit, and risk exit, but the current public dataset is empty and the website cannot yet distinguish Options Conviction evidence from generic weekly scanner output.
